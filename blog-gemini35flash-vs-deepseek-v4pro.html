Gemini 3.5 Flash vs DeepSeek V4 Pro: Google's Latest Meets Budget Champion

The Price Gap Is Staggering

Google just released Gemini 3.5 Flash at $1.50/$9.00 per 1M tokens. It's a solid mid-tier model with 1M context. But DeepSeek V4 Pro — which has been available for weeks — costs just $0.435/$0.87 with the same 1M context window.

When Gemini 3.5 Flash Makes Sense

Despite the massive price gap, Gemini 3.5 Flash has real advantages:

  • Google Cloud integration — Native support in Vertex AI, BigQuery ML, Cloud Functions
  • Enterprise compliance — SOC 2, HIPAA BAA, ISO 27001 certifications
  • Generous free tier — Experiment without spending a dime
  • US-based support — Enterprise SLAs and dedicated support channels
  • Multimodal capabilities — Native image and video understanding

When DeepSeek V4 Pro Wins

For most developers, DeepSeek V4 Pro is the obvious choice:

  • Cost-sensitive workloads — 89% cheaper at scale
  • High-volume applications — Chatbots, classification, content generation
  • Startups and indie devs — Every dollar counts
  • Open-weight flexibility — Self-host if needed

The Bottom Line

If you're building on Google Cloud and need enterprise compliance, Gemini 3.5 Flash is a solid choice. But for pure cost-efficiency, DeepSeek V4 Pro delivers comparable quality at a fraction of the price.

At 1,000 requests/day, you'd save over $1,900 per year by choosing DeepSeek V4 Pro over Gemini 3.5 Flash. That's real money for any team.
