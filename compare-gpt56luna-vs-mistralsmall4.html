The Verdict

Mistral Small 4 wins on price. GPT-5.6 Luna wins on context and quality.

Mistral Small 4 ($0.15/$0.60) is 85% cheaper on input and 90% cheaper on output than GPT-5.6 Luna ($1/$6). But Luna has an 8x larger context window (1.05M vs 128K tokens) and generally better performance on complex tasks. The right choice depends on whether you need long-context processing or just cheap, simple inference.

When to Choose Mistral Small 4

  • Ultra-high-volume workloads: At $0.15/$0.60 per 1M tokens, Mistral Small is the cheapest option for massive-scale processing. 100K requests/day costs just $14/mo.
  • Simple classification tasks: Intent detection, sentiment analysis, content moderation — these don't need large context or premium quality.
  • Prototype and MVP development: Build fast, iterate cheap. Upgrade to Luna or higher when you need more capability.
  • Budget-constrained projects: When every dollar matters and you can work within the 128K context limit.

When to Choose GPT-5.6 Luna

  • Long-document processing: Luna's 1.05M context window handles entire codebases, legal documents, or research papers that Mistral Small's 128K cannot.
  • Better quality on complex tasks: Luna generally outperforms Mistral Small on multi-step reasoning, code generation, and nuanced instruction following.
  • OpenAI ecosystem: If you're already using OpenAI's API and want to stay in their ecosystem with a budget option.
  • Hybrid cost optimization: Use Luna for tasks that need more than 128K context, route simpler tasks to Mistral Small.
