The Big Picture: Prices Have Plummeted

In just three years, the cost of using LLM APIs has dropped dramatically. What cost $60 per 1M tokens in 2023 now costs $0.10–$2.50. This isn't a gradual decline — it's a collapse.

The driving forces: competition between OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google; hardware improvements (better GPUs, optimized inference); open-source pressure from Meta's Llama and Mistral; and economies of scale as adoption grows.

Key Trends

2023
The GPT-4 Era
GPT-4 launched at $30/$60 per 1M tokens. Only OpenAI and Anthropic competed. Context windows were 8K-32K. Most developers used GPT-3.5 at $2.00 to save costs.
Early 2024
Price Wars Begin
Google enters with Gemini, forcing prices down. OpenAI cuts GPT-4 to $10/$30. Anthropic launches Claude 3 with competitive pricing. Context windows hit 128K.
Mid 2024
The Budget Revolution
GPT-4o mini at $0.15/$0.60 makes AI affordable for everyone. Claude Haiku, Gemini Flash compete at budget tier. Open-source Llama models pressure the entire market.
2025
Context Windows Explode
Google launches 1M context window. Anthropic hits 200K. OpenAI reaches 256K. Prices continue dropping. Embedding costs collapse to $0.02/1M tokens.
2026
The Current State
7 major providers compete. Budget models at $0.10/1M input tokens. 1M+ context windows. Prices 90% lower than 2023. AI is cheaper than ever.

What This Means for You

  • AI is now affordable for startups: What cost $1,000/month in 2023 now costs $50-100/month
  • Context windows are generous: You can process entire codebases and documents without chunking
  • Competition benefits you: With 7 providers competing, prices will keep dropping
  • Switching is easy: Most providers offer similar APIs — you can switch for better pricing
  • Budget models are production-ready: GPT-4o mini, Gemini Flash, and Haiku handle most tasks well

What to Expect Next

  • More price cuts: Competition will continue driving prices down through 2026-2027
  • Larger context windows: 5M+ tokens will become standard
  • Better budget models: Budget models will match 2024 flagship quality
  • Specialized models: Purpose-built models for code, math, and reasoning
  • Open-source parity: Open models will close the gap with proprietary ones
