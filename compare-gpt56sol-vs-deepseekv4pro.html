The Verdict

DeepSeek V4 Pro wins on price. GPT-5.6 Sol wins on quality.

DeepSeek V4 Pro ($0.43/$0.87) is 91% cheaper on input and 97% cheaper on output than GPT-5.6 Sol ($5/$30). For a standard request, DeepSeek costs $0.001 vs Sol's $0.0195 — a 95% savings. The question isn't whether DeepSeek is cheaper, but whether Sol's premium quality justifies 12-35x the cost for your specific use case.

When to Choose DeepSeek V4 Pro

  • High-volume, cost-sensitive workloads: When you're processing thousands of requests daily and every fraction of a cent matters. DeepSeek's 95% savings transform $585/mo into $30/mo.
  • Classification and routing: Simple tasks like intent classification, sentiment analysis, or request routing don't need premium quality. DeepSeek handles these well at a fraction of the cost.
  • Prototype and MVP development: Build and iterate fast without burning budget on API costs. Upgrade to Sol when you need production-quality output.
  • Data extraction and summarization: For structured data extraction, document summarization, and similar tasks, DeepSeek provides excellent value.

When to Choose GPT-5.6 Sol

  • Customer-facing applications: When output quality directly impacts user experience, reputation, or revenue. Premium models produce more polished, accurate, and nuanced responses.
  • Complex reasoning and analysis: Multi-step reasoning, mathematical proofs, legal analysis, or scientific research where accuracy is critical.
  • Code generation: For production code that needs to be correct, maintainable, and secure, Sol's quality advantage can save debugging time that offsets the cost premium.
  • Brand voice and creative content: Marketing copy, creative writing, or brand-sensitive communications where the difference in quality is noticeable.
