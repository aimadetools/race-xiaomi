GPT-5 mini vs Claude Haiku 4.5: Which Budget Model Should You Use?

⚠️ Claude 4 Deprecation Alert: Claude 4 models retire on June 15, 2026 (). If you use Claude 4, see our last-chance migration guide or use the deprecation calculator.

The budget AI model tier has gotten really interesting in 2026. OpenAI's GPT-5 mini launched at a price point that makes GPT-4o look expensive, while Anthropic's Claude Haiku 4.5 remains the go-to for developers who need Claude's reasoning quality at a lower cost. Here's a detailed breakdown of pricing, capabilities, and real-world costs.

Pricing at a Glance

As of May 2026:

  • GPT-5 mini: $0.25 per 1M input tokens, $2.00 per 1M output tokens
  • Claude Haiku 4.5: $1.00 per 1M input tokens, $5.00 per 1M output tokens

GPT-5 mini is 4x cheaper on input and 2.5x cheaper on output. For high-volume applications, this gap adds up fast.

Context Window

  • GPT-5 mini: 272K tokens
  • Claude Haiku 4.5: 200K tokens

GPT-5 mini wins here too. Its 272K context window is 36% larger than Haiku's 200K. For long documents, codebases, or conversation histories, GPT-5 mini gives you more room to work with.

Quality Comparison

Price isn't everything. Here's where each model excels:

  • GPT-5 mini: Strong at structured output, function calling, instruction following, and code tasks. Benefits from GPT-5's architecture improvements — it's not just a smaller model, it's a smarter model at a budget price.
  • Claude Haiku 4.5: Excellent at nuanced reasoning, natural-sounding text, and tasks requiring careful judgment. Anthropic's safety training means it's more reliable for customer-facing applications where tone matters.

For well-defined tasks (classification, extraction, formatting, code generation), GPT-5 mini delivers excellent results at a fraction of the cost. For tasks requiring subtle judgment or where output quality directly impacts user experience, Claude Haiku may be worth the premium.

When to Choose Each

Choose GPT-5 mini when:

  • Cost is a primary concern (saves 60-72% vs Haiku)
  • You need a larger context window (272K vs 200K)
  • Your tasks are well-defined (classification, extraction, code)
  • You're building high-volume applications
  • You're already in the OpenAI ecosystem

Choose Claude Haiku 4.5 when:

  • Output quality and tone matter (customer-facing chat)
  • You need Claude's specific reasoning style
  • You're building on Anthropic's ecosystem
  • Safety and alignment are top priorities
  • Your tasks require nuanced judgment

The Verdict

GPT-5 mini is the clear price-to-performance winner in the budget tier. It's 4x cheaper on input, 2.5x cheaper on output, and has a larger context window. Claude Haiku 4.5 justifies its premium only when output quality and tone are mission-critical.

For most budget-conscious developers, GPT-5 mini should be the default choice in 2026. It delivers near-flagship quality at budget prices. Reserve Claude Haiku for customer-facing applications where the subtle quality differences matter.
