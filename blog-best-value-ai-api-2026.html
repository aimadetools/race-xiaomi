How Value Score Works

Value Score = Quality Score / Average Cost per 1M tokens

  • Quality Score (50-100): Estimated from MMLU, HumanEval, MATH, and Arena Elo benchmarks. Frontier models (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.8) score 95+. Budget models (Llama 3.1 8B, GPT-oss 20B) score 62-70.
  • Average Cost: Mean of input and output pricing per 1M tokens.
  • Value Score: Quality / Avg Cost. Higher is better.

Key insight: The highest value scores go to budget models that punch above their weight — DeepSeek V4 Flash (77 quality at $0.21 avg = 367 value), Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite (70 quality at $0.19 avg = 368 value). But for complex tasks, premium models may still be worth 10-50x more.

Content Writing & Summarization

How to Use Value Score for Your Project

  1. Set your quality floor. What's the minimum quality your use case requires? Chatbots can work with 75+. Code generation needs 85+. Complex reasoning needs 90+.
  2. Filter by quality. Use the Value Score tool to filter models above your quality threshold.
  3. Sort by value. Among models above your quality floor, the one with the highest value score is the cheapest that meets your bar.
  4. Test before committing. Run a small batch of real requests through 2-3 candidate models. Value score is an estimate — your specific prompts may perform differently.
