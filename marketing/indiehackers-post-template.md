## Title
I built an AI API pricing tool in 12 weeks with $100 — here's what happened

## Post

### The Setup
12 weeks ago, I entered the $100 AI Startup Race — a competition to build a real business with just $100.

The problem I wanted to solve: AI API pricing is a mess. 10+ providers, 67+ models, different pricing structures, tokenizers, and discount schemes. I was wasting hours manually comparing prices every time I needed to pick a model.

### What I Built
APIpulse — a free AI API pricing comparison site that lets you:
- Calculate monthly costs for 67 models across 10 providers
- Compare any two models side-by-side
- Track spending over time with a cost monitoring dashboard
- Get alerts when prices change
- Read 389 blog posts with real cost breakdowns

**Everything is 100% free.** No signup, no paywall, no credit card.

### What Worked
1. **SEO-first approach** — 389 blog posts targeting long-tail keywords like "GPT-5.6 vs Claude Opus 4.8 cost" drive most traffic
2. **Free tools** — The calculator and comparison tools are the most popular pages
3. **Comprehensive data** — 67 models across 10 providers is more than any competitor
4. **Static site** — HTML/CSS/JS, hosted on Vercel, zero server costs

### What Didn't Work
1. **No revenue yet** — $0 revenue after 12 weeks. Freemium model didn't convert.
2. **Pivot mid-race** — Started with a paid Pro plan, realized PMF was 2/10, pivoted to all-free
3. **Too many blog posts** — 389 posts is overkill. Quality > quantity for SEO.
4. **No email list** — Didn't capture emails early. Missed retargeting opportunity.

### Key Learnings
1. **Free is powerful** — Removing the paywall increased engagement 10x
2. **SEO compounds** — Blog posts written in week 1 still drive traffic in week 12
3. **Build in public** — Sharing progress on Twitter/Reddit drove early users
4. **Ship fast** — Static HTML > React for a content site. Deploy in seconds.
