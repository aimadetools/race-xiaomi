Why Marketing Token Costs Matter

AI marketing content has a different token profile than simple Q&A or support. Every marketing piece includes:

  • Brand context — voice guidelines, target audience, campaign goals (~400-800 tokens)
  • Brief + research — product details, competitor info, SEO keywords (~600-1,200 tokens)
  • Output — the generated content (blog post, ad copy, landing page) (~500-1,200 tokens)

A typical marketing piece uses ~2,000 input tokens and ~800 output tokens. That's 3x the input of a simple email but only 2.5x the output. The cost difference at scale is significant: DeepSeek V4 Flash costs $151/month while Claude Sonnet 4.6 costs $540/month for 100 pieces per day.

The key cost insight: marketing content quality directly impacts revenue. A 1% improvement in ad click-through rate or blog engagement is worth far more than the model cost difference. Use premium models for high-visibility content (landing pages, pillar blog posts, hero ad copy). Use budget models for high-volume variants (A/B test copies, social media drafts, product descriptions).

Frequently Asked Questions About AI Marketing Content Costs

What is the cheapest AI model for marketing content in 2026?
DeepSeek V4 Flash is the cheapest AI model for marketing content at $0.00050 per piece (2,000 input + 800 output tokens). At 100 pieces per day, that's roughly $151/month. Llama 4 Scout ($0.00083/piece) is also cheap and is self-hostable for unlimited content at no per-token cost.
How much does AI marketing content cost per piece?
A typical marketing content piece uses ~2,000 input tokens (brand context + brief + research) and ~800 output tokens (the content). On DeepSeek V4 Flash, that costs $0.00050 per piece. On GPT-5 mini, it costs $0.00210. On Claude Sonnet 4.6, it costs $0.01800. At 100 pieces/day, costs range from $151/mo (DeepSeek) to $540/mo (Claude Sonnet 4.6).
Which AI model writes the best ad copy?
Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3.00/$15.00 per 1M) produces the most persuasive, emotionally resonant ad copy with strong hooks and CTAs. GPT-5 ($1.25/$10.00) excels at data-driven ad copy with specific claims and social proof. GPT-5 mini ($0.25/$2.00) offers the best balance for high-volume ad variants where testing volume matters more than per-piece perfection.
Can AI write good blog posts for marketing?
Yes, but quality varies by model and use case. Claude Sonnet 4.6 writes the most engaging, well-structured blog posts with natural flow. GPT-5 produces thorough, research-backed content. Budget models like GPT-5 mini can handle first drafts, listicles, and product descriptions well. For SEO content at scale, a hybrid approach works best: premium models for pillar content, budget models for supporting articles.
How many marketing pieces can AI generate per dollar?
On DeepSeek V4 Flash, $1 handles about 2,000 marketing pieces. On Llama 4 Scout, $1 handles about 1,200 pieces. On GPT-5 mini, $1 handles about 476 pieces. On Claude Haiku 4.5, $1 handles about 167 pieces. On Claude Sonnet 4.6, $1 handles about 56 pieces. Budget models produce 36x more content per dollar than premium models.
Is AI-generated marketing content good for SEO?
Google's guidance states AI-generated content is not inherently penalized — what matters is quality and helpfulness. Premium models like Claude Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5 produce content that reads naturally and provides genuine value, which performs well in search. Budget models may produce more generic content that needs human editing. The best approach: use AI for research, outlines, and drafts, then add human expertise, original data, and unique perspectives.
