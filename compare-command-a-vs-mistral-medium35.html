Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mistral Medium 3.5 cheaper than Command A?

Yes. Mistral Medium 3.5 costs $1.50/M input and $7.50/M output. Command A costs $2.50/M input and $10.00/M output. Mistral Medium 3.5 is 40% cheaper on input and 25% cheaper on output. For a typical workload of 1M input + 500K output tokens/month, Mistral Medium 3.5 costs $5.25 vs Command A's $7.50 — saving you $2.25/month (30%).

Is Command A better than Mistral Medium 3.5?

Both have 128K token context windows, making them suitable for enterprise use. Command A from Cohere excels at RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) and enterprise search scenarios, with strong grounding and citation capabilities. Mistral Medium 3.5 from Mistral is stronger at general-purpose tasks and coding, with broad language support. The best choice depends on whether your priority is enterprise search (Command A) or general versatility (Mistral Medium 3.5).

When should I choose Command A over Mistral Medium 3.5?

Choose Command A when you need: (1) enterprise RAG and search with grounded, citation-backed answers, (2) Cohere's embedding and reranking ecosystem for knowledge-intensive workflows, (3) optimized performance on document-heavy enterprise tasks. Choose Mistral Medium 3.5 when you want lower cost, general-purpose versatility, strong coding ability, or need broad multilingual support.

What are the context windows for Command A and Mistral Medium 3.5?

Both Command A and Mistral Medium 3.5 offer 128K token context windows. This is sufficient for processing long documents, multi-turn conversations, and complex RAG pipelines. For use cases requiring larger contexts (1M+ tokens), you would need to look at models like Claude Opus 4.8 or DeepSeek V4 Pro.
