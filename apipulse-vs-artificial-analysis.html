When to use APIpulse

APIpulse is built for developers who are actively implementing AI APIs and need to make cost decisions. It's the right tool when you need to:

  • Calculate exact monthly costs for a specific model and usage pattern
  • Find cheaper alternatives to your current model — ranked by savings
  • Get migration code to switch from one provider to another
  • Generate cost reports for your team, manager, or procurement
  • Embed live pricing in your docs or README with one script tag
  • Track price changes across providers over time

When to use Artificial Analysis

Artificial Analysis is the right choice when your focus is on model quality and performance rather than just pricing:

  • Benchmarking model quality — comparing accuracy, reasoning, and output quality
  • Measuring latency and throughput — real-world performance metrics
  • Evaluating newer models — they cover more models including experimental ones
  • Provider comparison — side-by-side quality vs cost tradeoffs

Best practice: use both

Many developers use Artificial Analysis to narrow down which models have the quality they need, then use APIpulse to calculate exact costs and find the cheapest provider for that quality tier. They're complementary tools.

For example: Artificial Analysis might show that Claude Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5.5 have similar quality for your use case. APIpulse then shows you that Sonnet 4.6 costs $3/$15 per 1M tokens while GPT-5.5 costs $5/$30 — making Sonnet 4.6 the better value.
