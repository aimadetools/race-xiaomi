Frequently Asked Questions

Is Google Gemini cheaper than Mistral for API usage?

Yes, Google Gemini is significantly cheaper than Mistral across all tiers. Gemini Flash Lite ($0.075/$0.30 per 1M tokens) is half the input price of Mistral Small 4 ($0.15/$0.60). Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite ($0.10/$0.40) is also cheaper than Mistral Small 4. Even at the mid-tier, Gemini 2.5 Pro ($1.25/$5.00) offers more value than Mistral Large 3 ($0.50/$1.50) due to its 1M context window. Google wins on pure cost at every tier.

What is the cheapest model between Mistral and Google?

GPT-oss 20B at $0.08/$0.35 is the cheapest option overall — half the input price of Mistral Small 4 ($0.15/$0.60). At 1M tokens/month, Gemini Flash Lite costs $0.75 on input vs $1.50 for Mistral Small 4. Google also offers Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite at $0.10/$0.40, which is still cheaper than any Mistral model.

Does Google Gemini have a larger context window than Mistral?

Yes, significantly. All Google Gemini models support 1M token context windows. Mistral Large 3 and Mistral Small 4 are limited to 128K tokens — 8x smaller. For long document analysis, codebase processing, or any task requiring large context, Google Gemini is the clear winner at a lower price point.

Mistral Large 3 vs Gemini 2.5 Pro: which should I choose?

Gemini 2.5 Pro ($1.25/$5.00) is more expensive on input than Mistral Large 3 ($0.50/$1.50), but offers 8x larger context (1M vs 128K) and stronger reasoning capabilities. For tasks that need large context or advanced reasoning, Gemini 2.5 Pro is worth the premium. For simple, high-volume tasks where context window doesn't matter, Mistral Large 3 is cheaper. Choose Mistral only if you need EU data sovereignty.

When should I choose Mistral over Google Gemini?

Choose Mistral when you need European data sovereignty for GDPR compliance. Mistral is a French company with EU-hosted infrastructure, which matters for sensitive workloads under EU regulation. For all other use cases — cost optimization, context window, model variety — Google Gemini is the better choice. Google offers 4 models across budget and premium tiers, all with 1M context windows.
