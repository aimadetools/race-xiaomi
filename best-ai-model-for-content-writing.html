Best Model by Content Type

Different writing tasks need different strengths

Blog Posts

SEO-optimized articles, listicles, how-to guides. Needs clear structure, good readability, and keyword integration.

GPT-5 ($1.25/$10) — best at following SEO prompts and structured writing. DeepSeek V4 Pro for budget.

Marketing Copy

Landing pages, ad copy, email campaigns. Persuasion and emotional resonance matter most.

Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3/$15) — excels at persuasive, brand-aware writing. GPT-5 for ad-specific copy.

Social Media

Short-form posts, threads, captions. Needs punchy writing with personality. High volume, low cost tolerance.

DeepSeek V4 Pro ($0.435/$0.87) or GPT-5 mini ($0.25/$2) — fast and cheap for short-form.

Long-Form Content

E-books, whitepapers, guides (3,000+ words). Needs coherence, depth, and consistent voice throughout.

Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3/$15) — best long-context coherence. GPT-5 for structured long-form.

Technical Writing

Documentation, API references, tutorials. Accuracy and clarity are critical.

GPT-5 ($1.25/$10) — most accurate for technical content. Gemini 3.1 Pro for code-heavy docs.

Product Descriptions

E-commerce listings, feature descriptions, comparison pages. High volume with template-based variation.

GPT-5 mini ($0.25/$2) or Llama 4 Scout ($0.18/$0.59) — cheap and sufficient for templated content.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI model for content writing?
GPT-5 ($1.25/$10.00 per 1M tokens) is the best overall AI model for content writing, producing the highest quality prose and following complex prompts accurately. Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3.00/$15.00) excels at nuanced, long-form writing and is the top choice for editorial content. For budget-conscious writers, DeepSeek V4 Pro ($0.435/$0.87) delivers surprisingly good quality at 65% less than GPT-5.
What is the cheapest AI for blog writing?
The cheapest AI models for blog writing are Llama 4 Scout ($0.18/$0.59 per 1M tokens) and GPT-5 mini ($0.25/$2.00). Llama 4 Scout costs roughly $0.01-0.03 per 1,000-word blog post. However, for publish-ready quality without heavy editing, DeepSeek V4 Pro ($0.435/$0.87) is the cheapest option that rarely needs significant rewrites.
How much does it cost to write a blog post with AI?
A typical 1,500-word blog post requires roughly 2,500 input tokens and 2,000 output tokens. Cost by model: Llama 4 Scout: ~$0.02. GPT-5 mini: ~$0.04. DeepSeek V4 Pro: ~$0.03. GPT-5: ~$0.23. Claude Sonnet 4.6: ~$0.38. At 40 posts/month, costs range from $0.68/month (Llama 4 Scout) to $9.25/month (GPT-5) to $15.00/month (Claude Sonnet 4.6).
Can AI replace content writers?
AI models in 2026 can produce publish-ready first drafts for blog posts, product descriptions, and social media content. GPT-5 and Claude Sonnet 4.6 write at a quality level that is often indistinguishable from human first drafts. However, AI still benefits from human editing for brand voice consistency, fact-checking, and creative storytelling. Most teams use AI for 70-80% of content creation, with humans handling editing and final polish.
Which AI model is best for marketing copy?
For marketing copy where persuasion and brand voice matter most: Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3.00/$15.00) excels at nuanced, persuasive writing. GPT-5 ($1.25/$10.00) is strong for ad copy and email campaigns. For high-volume marketing on a budget, DeepSeek V4 Pro ($0.435/$0.87) produces competent marketing copy at a fraction of the cost.
