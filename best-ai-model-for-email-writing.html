Why Email Token Costs Matter

AI email generation has a predictable token structure. Every email includes:

  • System prompt — brand voice, tone guidelines, recipient context (~200-400 tokens)
  • User context — recipient info, campaign details, previous interactions (~100-300 tokens)
  • Output — the generated email body, subject line, and preview (~200-400 tokens)

A typical email uses ~600 input tokens and ~300 output tokens. That's small per email, but at scale — 1,000 emails per day — the cost difference is dramatic. DeepSeek V4 Flash costs $25/month while Claude Sonnet 4.6 costs $567/month for the same volume.

The key cost insight: email quality directly impacts open rates and conversions. A 1% improvement in open rate on 100K emails/month is worth far more than the model cost difference. Use premium models for high-value cold outreach and marketing emails. Use budget models for transactional emails (receipts, notifications, password resets) where template-based generation works well.

Best Model by Email Use Case

Different email types have different quality and volume requirements

Cold Outreach Sequences

Personalized sales emails that need to sound human, avoid spam filters, and drive replies. Quality directly impacts response rates.

Claude Sonnet 4.6 — most natural, human-sounding copy that avoids AI detection

Marketing Newsletters

Weekly or monthly content emails with consistent brand voice. Needs engaging subject lines and clear CTAs at high volume.

GPT-5 mini — strong marketing copy at 8x less cost than premium models

Transactional Emails

Order confirmations, password resets, shipping updates. Template-based with variable substitution. Quality bar is clarity, not persuasion.

DeepSeek V4 Flash — fastest, cheapest, handles templated generation perfectly

Customer Follow-ups

Post-support or post-purchase follow-up emails. Needs personalization and empathy but is high volume.

GPT-5 mini — good empathy and personalization at budget cost

Drip Campaigns

Automated multi-step email sequences. Each email builds on previous context. Needs consistent tone across the sequence.

Claude Haiku 4.5 — good quality at mid-range cost for sequential content

A/B Test Variants

Generating multiple subject line and body variants for testing. High volume, needs diversity in output. Quality per variant is less critical.

DeepSeek V4 Flash — cheap enough to generate 10 variants per test

Frequently Asked Questions About AI Email Writing Costs

What is the cheapest AI model for email writing in 2026?
DeepSeek V4 Flash is the cheapest AI model for email writing at $0.00028 per email (600 input + 300 output tokens). At 1,000 emails per day, that's roughly $25/month. Llama 4 Scout ($0.00028/email) is similarly cheap and is self-hostable for unlimited emails at no per-token cost.
How much does AI email generation cost per email?
A typical AI-generated email uses ~600 input tokens (system prompt + context + instructions) and ~300 output tokens (the email body). On DeepSeek V4 Flash, that costs $0.00028 per email. On GPT-5 mini, it costs $0.00075. On Claude Sonnet 4.6, it costs $0.00630. At 1,000 emails/day, costs range from $25/mo (DeepSeek) to $567/mo (Claude Sonnet 4.6).
Which AI model writes the best marketing emails?
Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3.00/$15.00 per 1M) writes the most natural, compelling marketing emails with strong subject lines and clear CTAs. GPT-5 ($1.25/$10.00) excels at persuasive copy with data-driven arguments. GPT-5 mini ($0.25/$2.00) offers the best balance for high-volume transactional emails where quality matters but budget is tight.
Can AI write effective cold outreach emails?
Yes, but quality varies significantly. Claude Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5 produce the most personalized, natural-sounding cold emails that avoid spam filters. Budget models like DeepSeek V4 Flash can work for templated outreach with variable substitution, but may produce generic-sounding copy. The best approach: use premium models to generate templates, then budget models for personalization at scale.
How many emails can AI write per dollar?
On DeepSeek V4 Flash, $1 handles about 3,571 emails. On Llama 4 Scout, $1 handles about 3,571 emails. On GPT-5 mini, $1 handles about 1,333 emails. On Claude Haiku 4.5, $1 handles about 385 emails. On Claude Sonnet 4.6, $1 handles about 159 emails. Budget models produce 20x more emails per dollar than premium models.
Is AI-generated email content detectable?
AI-generated emails can sometimes be detected by pattern analysis, especially for generic content. Premium models like Claude Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5 produce more natural, varied text that is harder to distinguish from human writing. Budget models tend to use more repetitive patterns. For best results, use AI for drafting, then add personal touches and human review before sending.
