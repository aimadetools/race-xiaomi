Frequently Asked Questions

How much cheaper is GPT-5 mini than Claude Sonnet 4.6?

GPT-5 mini costs $0.25/$2 per 1M tokens while Claude Sonnet 4.6 costs $3/$15 per 1M tokens. GPT-5 mini is 92% cheaper on input and 87% cheaper on output. For a typical workload, GPT-5 mini costs $4.50/mo vs Sonnet 4.6's $57/mo — a 92% savings.

Is GPT-5 mini as good as Claude Sonnet 4.6?

Claude Sonnet 4.6 is Anthropic's mid-tier model with 1M context window, excelling at complex reasoning and coding tasks. GPT-5 mini is OpenAI's budget model optimized for cost-efficiency with 272K context. For most common tasks (chatbots, content generation, data extraction), GPT-5 mini delivers excellent results at 87-92% lower cost. Sonnet 4.6 excels at tasks requiring larger context windows and more nuanced reasoning.

When should I choose Claude Sonnet 4.6 over GPT-5 mini?

Choose Claude Sonnet 4.6 when you need: (1) 1M context window for long documents, (2) Superior coding and reasoning quality, (3) Tasks where output quality directly impacts user experience. Choose GPT-5 mini for high-volume, cost-sensitive workloads where good-enough quality at 87-92% lower cost is the priority.

Can I mix Claude Sonnet 4.6 and GPT-5 mini?

Yes. Route simple, high-volume tasks (chatbots, content generation, data extraction) to GPT-5 mini at $0.25/$2, and reserve Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3/$15) for complex reasoning and tasks requiring large context windows. This tiered strategy can reduce costs by 80-90% while maintaining quality where it matters most.
