What Changed in July 2026

July brought the biggest wave of model launches and deprecations since the Claude 4 shutdown. Claude Sonnet 5 arrived at aggressive intro pricing, OpenAI replaced the entire GPT-4o family with GPT-4.1, and Mistral entered the ultra-budget tier.

Claude Sonnet 5 — The New Mid-Tier King

Anthropic launched Claude Sonnet 5 at $2/$10 per 1M tokens (intro pricing through Aug 31, regular $3/$15). With a 1M context window and quality matching Claude Sonnet 4.6, it's the best mid-tier value right now. The intro price makes it cheaper than GPT-4.1 ($2/$8) on output tokens — a first for Anthropic.

GPT-4.1 Replaces GPT-4o

OpenAI officially deprecated GPT-4o and GPT-4o mini. The GPT-4.1 family is 20% cheaper with 8× larger context (1M vs 128K). GPT-4.1 ($2/$8), GPT-4.1 mini ($0.40/$1.60), and GPT-4.1 nano ($0.10/$0.40) span the full price range. Note: GPT-4.1 nano is already marked for shutdown on Oct 23, 2026.

GPT-5 and GPT-5 Mini Deprecated

OpenAI deprecated GPT-5 ($1.25/$10) and GPT-5 mini ($0.25/$2) with a shutdown date of Dec 11, 2026. Migrate to GPT-5.5 ($5/$30) for premium or GPT-5.4 mini ($0.40/$1.60) for budget. GPT-oss 20B at $0.08/$0.35 remains the cheapest OpenAI-hosted option.

Mistral Small 4 — Ultra-Budget contender

Mistral launched Small 4 at $0.15/$0.60 per 1M tokens with 128K context. It's price-competitive with DeepSeek V4 Flash ($0.14/$0.28) and significantly cheaper than Jamba Mini ($0.20/$0.40). A strong option for EU data residency requirements.

Together.ai Model Cleanup

Together.ai delisted Llama 4 Scout and Maverick from serverless inference in July 2026. Llama 3.1 70B and 8B were also removed. The recommended replacement is Llama 3.3 70B at $1.04/$1.04. These models may still be available via dedicated endpoints.
