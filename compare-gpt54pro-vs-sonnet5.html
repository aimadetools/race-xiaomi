Frequently Asked Questions

Is GPT-5.4 Pro or Claude Sonnet 5 cheaper?

Claude Sonnet 5 is dramatically cheaper. It costs $3.00/$15.00 per 1M tokens while GPT-5.4 Pro costs $30.00/$180.00. That's 90% cheaper on input and 91.7% cheaper on output. At 1M tokens/month, Sonnet 5 costs ~$54 vs GPT-5.4 Pro's $2,100.

What is GPT-5.4 Pro used for?

GPT-5.4 Pro at $30/$180 is OpenAI's most capable model, designed for the most complex reasoning tasks — advanced mathematics, multi-step research, complex code generation. It's 10x more expensive than Sonnet 5 on input.

When should I use GPT-5.4 Pro vs Claude Sonnet 5?

GPT-5.4 Pro ($30/$180) is for tasks requiring absolute maximum capability where cost is secondary. Claude Sonnet 5 ($3/$15) handles most premium tasks at 90% lower cost with a larger 1M context window. Most users should start with Sonnet 5.

Does Claude Sonnet 5 have a larger context window?

Yes. Claude Sonnet 5 has a 1M token context window, while GPT-5.4 Pro has 400K. That's 2.5x larger. For tasks requiring processing large documents, codebases, or datasets, Sonnet 5 offers both lower cost and larger context.

Can I use both models together?

Yes! A cost-effective pattern: use Claude Sonnet 5 ($3/$15) for 95% of tasks and route only the most complex, high-stakes tasks to GPT-5.4 Pro ($30/$180). This gives you maximum capability where needed while keeping costs 90%+ lower.
