Frequently Asked Questions

Is Gemini cheaper than Claude for API usage?

Yes, Gemini is significantly cheaper than Claude across all tiers. Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite ($0.075/$0.30 per 1M tokens) is 92% cheaper on input and 94% cheaper on output than Claude Haiku 4.5 ($1/$5). Gemini 2.5 Pro ($1.25/$10) is 58% cheaper on input and 33% cheaper on output than Claude Sonnet 4.6 ($3/$15). For budget-conscious developers, Gemini offers excellent value with 1M context windows on all models.

What is the cheapest Gemini model vs the cheapest Claude model?

GPT-oss 20B at $0.08/$0.35 is the cheapest. Gemini Flash Lite is 92% cheaper on input and 94% cheaper on output. At 1M tokens/month, Gemini Flash Lite costs $0.75+$3=$3.75 vs Claude Haiku's $10+$50=$60 — saving $56.25/month.

Which has a bigger context window: Gemini or Claude?

Both offer 1M token context windows. All four Gemini models (Flash Lite, Flash, 2.5 Pro, 3.1 Pro) support 1M tokens. Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Opus 4.7 also support 1M tokens. However, Gemini's budget models (Flash Lite at $0.075/M) include 1M context, while Claude's budget model (Haiku 4.5 at $1/M) only has 200K context.

Claude Sonnet 4.6 vs Gemini 2.5 Pro: which should I choose?

Gemini 2.5 Pro is 58% cheaper on input ($1.25 vs $3) and 33% cheaper on output ($10 vs $15), with a larger 1M context window vs Claude Sonnet 4.6's 200K. Choose Gemini 2.5 Pro for cost-sensitive workloads, long-context tasks, and startups. Choose Claude Sonnet 4.6 when you need Anthropic's safety guarantees or specific Claude capabilities.

Is Gemini Flash good enough for production use?

Yes, Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite is excellent for production use at $0.10/$0.40 per 1M tokens with 1M context. It handles chatbots, content generation, summarization, and most NLP tasks well. Gemini Flash Lite ($0.075/$0.30) is even cheaper for simpler tasks. For complex reasoning, upgrade to Gemini 2.5 Pro or Claude Sonnet 4.6.
