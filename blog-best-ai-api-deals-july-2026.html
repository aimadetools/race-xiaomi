1. Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite — $0.10/M Input

The absolute cheapest model with a 1M context window. At $0.10 input and $0.40 output, Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite is Google's answer to high-volume, cost-sensitive workloads. It handles text, images, and video inputs.

Best for: Content moderation, classification, embedding-like tasks, high-volume chatbot backends — anywhere you're processing millions of tokens and don't need frontier reasoning.

Watch out: Output at $0.40/M is slightly more expensive than DeepSeek V4 Flash's $0.28/M. If your workload is output-heavy, DeepSeek wins.

2. DeepSeek V4 Flash — $0.14/M Input, $0.28/M Output

DeepSeek continues to dominate the price-performance ratio. V4 Flash at $0.14/$0.28 per 1M tokens with a 1M context window is absurdly cheap. It's the cheapest output pricing of any model we track.

Best for: Output-heavy tasks — content generation, summarization, code completion, translation. If you're generating more tokens than you're consuming, DeepSeek V4 Flash is the mathematically cheapest option.

The savings are real: Switching from GPT-5.5 ($5/$30) to DeepSeek V4 Flash saves 97% on input and 99% on output. At 1M tokens/day, that's $150/month vs $4,200/month.

3. Mistral Small 4 — $0.15/M Input

Mistral's Small 4 at $0.15/$0.60 per 1M tokens is the cheapest European model. With a 128K context window, it handles most workloads fine — and you get EU data sovereignty as a bonus.

Best for: EU-based companies with GDPR requirements, applications needing European data residency, and teams that want budget pricing without US cloud dependency.

Trade-off: 128K context vs 1M on DeepSeek and Gemini. If you need to process entire codebases in a single prompt, you'll need a larger-window model.

4. GPT-4.1 mini — $0.40/M Input (Best OpenAI Deal)

If you're locked into the OpenAI ecosystem, GPT-4.1 mini at $0.40/$1.60 per 1M tokens is the best deal they offer. It has a 1M context window and handles most tasks that GPT-5 can do — at 12.5x lower cost.

Best for: Teams already using OpenAI's SDK, assistants API, or fine-tuning pipeline. The ecosystem lock-in is real — if switching providers would take weeks of engineering, GPT-4.1 mini is your budget play.

vs GPT-5.5: Same ecosystem, 12.5x cheaper. Unless you specifically need GPT-5.5's advanced reasoning, mini is the smart choice.

5. DeepSeek V4 Pro — $0.44/M Input

When you need better quality than Flash but still want budget pricing, DeepSeek V4 Pro at $0.44/$0.87 per 1M tokens is the sweet spot. It's 3x more expensive than V4 Flash but noticeably better at complex tasks.

Best for: Tasks where Flash makes too many mistakes — code review, technical writing, multi-step reasoning. The quality jump from Flash to Pro is often worth the 3x price increase.

Still crazy cheap: Even at $0.44/M, DeepSeek V4 Pro is 11x cheaper than GPT-5.5 on input and 34x cheaper on output.

6. Gemini 2.5 Flash — $0.30/M Input (Google's Sweet Spot)

Gemini 2.5 Flash at $0.30/$2.50 per 1M tokens sits between Flash-Lite and Pro. It's the Goldilocks option for Google Cloud users — good quality, reasonable price, 1M context.

Best for: Google Cloud workloads that need better quality than Flash-Lite but don't want to pay Pro prices. Native multimodal support (text + images + video) is a bonus.

vs DeepSeek V4 Flash: Gemini is 2x more expensive on input but 9x more expensive on output. If you generate a lot of tokens, DeepSeek wins. If you consume a lot of input (RAG, long docs), they're comparable.

7. Claude Sonnet 5 — $2.00/M Input (Intro Pricing)

Anthropic's latest Sonnet at $2.00/$10.00 per 1M tokens (introductory pricing through August 31, 2026 — regular price will be $3/$15). It's the most expensive model on this list, but the quality is exceptional.

Best for: Tasks where quality matters more than cost — complex reasoning, nuanced writing, agentic workflows, and anything where cheap models produce too many errors. The 1M context window handles enterprise workloads.

Act fast: The $2/$10 intro pricing ends August 31. After that, it goes to $3/$15 — still competitive, but 50% more expensive on input.
