Marketing-Specific Considerations

  • Output quality matters: Marketing copy is customer-facing. A bad product description loses sales. A bad ad wastes budget. Don't always pick the cheapest — test output quality on real campaigns first.
  • A/B testing needs volume: AI excels at generating 20 ad variants for A/B testing. The marginal cost of one more variant is near zero. Use this to run more experiments.
  • Brand voice needs fine-tuning: Generic AI output sounds generic. For consistent brand voice, provide style guides in the prompt (input tokens) or fine-tune a small model. Fine-tuning costs more upfront but produces better output per-token.
  • SEO content is input-heavy: Blog posts and SEO pages need keyword research, competitor analysis, and outline as input. The input-to-output ratio is higher than social media content.
  • Multi-channel from one prompt: One product brief can generate email, ad, social, and blog variants. Structure your prompts to produce multi-format output from a single API call to reduce per-channel costs.
