Frequently Asked Questions

Is DeepSeek V3.2 cheaper than GPT-5 mini?

Yes, DeepSeek V3.2 is slightly cheaper on input and significantly cheaper on output. DeepSeek V3.2 costs $0.23/M input and $0.34/M output, while GPT-5 mini costs $0.25/M input and $2/M output. DeepSeek V3.2 is 8% cheaper on input and 83% cheaper on output tokens.

Which has a larger context window?

GPT-5 mini has a much larger context window at 272K tokens compared to DeepSeek V3.2's 128K tokens. If your use case involves processing moderately long documents or conversations, GPT-5 mini gives you 2.1x more context.

When should I choose DeepSeek V3.2 over GPT-5 mini?

Choose DeepSeek V3.2 when you need: (1) Maximum cost savings (83% cheaper output), (2) Tasks where DeepSeek's training gives competitive results, (3) Projects that can leverage open-source models. For most use cases, GPT-5 mini offers better value per dollar due to larger context.

Can I mix DeepSeek V3.2 and GPT-5 mini to optimize costs?

Yes. Use DeepSeek V3.2 for most requests (83% cheaper output) and route long-context tasks (>128K tokens) to GPT-5 mini. This multi-model strategy can save 40-60% vs using GPT-5 mini for everything.
