I Launched on Product Hunt — Here's My Waitlist Strategy

Case Study January 20, 2024 · 7 min read

Last month, I launched my side project on Product Hunt. I ended up as the #3 Product of the Day with 847 upvotes and 2,341 waitlist signups.

But here's the thing: I didn't just show up on launch day and hope for the best.

I spent 4 weeks building a waitlist before launching. That waitlist became the foundation of my entire launch strategy. Here's exactly what I did.

#3 Product of Day

The Problem: Zero Audience

Like most indie hackers, I had no audience. My Twitter had 200 followers (mostly bots). My email list was empty. I was building in a vacuum.

I knew that Product Hunt success depends on Day 1 momentum. And momentum comes from having people ready to support you from minute one.

So I decided to build a waitlist. Not just any waitlist — a viral waitlist where every signup helps bring in more signups.

Week 1: Setting Up the Foundation

Day 1-2: Built the waitlist page

Created a simple landing page with my product name, a one-line description, and an email signup form. Nothing fancy — just enough to capture interest.

Day 3: Added referral tracking

This was the game-changer. Every signup got a unique referral link. Share it with friends, move up the list. Suddenly, my users were doing the marketing for me.

Day 4-5: First shares

Posted on Twitter and Indie Hackers. Got my first 50 signups. But the magic happened when those 50 people started sharing their referral links.

Day 6-7: Viral loop kicks in

By end of week 1, I had 287 signups. The referral system was working — about 30% of signups were coming from referrals, not my posts.

Key insight: The first 50 signups are the hardest. After that, the referral loop takes over. Focus all your energy on getting those first 50.

Week 2: Building Momentum

With the foundation in place, I focused on two things: content and community.

Content Strategy

I wrote three pieces of content:

  • "Building in Public" thread on Twitter — Shared my journey, metrics, and learnings. This alone brought 200+ signups.
  • Reddit post on r/SideProject — Honest post about what I was building and why. Got 150 upvotes and 300 signups.
  • Indie Hackers update — Posted weekly progress. The IH community is incredibly supportive of builders.

Community Engagement

I replied to every single comment and DM. When someone signed up, I sent a personal welcome email asking what feature they were most excited about.

This did two things:

  1. Made people feel valued (increasing their likelihood to share)
  2. Gave me product feedback to prioritize features

End of Week 2: 847 signups, 41% from referrals.

Week 3: The Scarcity Play

I noticed signups were slowing down. So I introduced scarcity:

  • Added a "Only 500 spots left for early access" banner
  • Showed a progress bar filling up
  • Announced that top 10 referrers would get lifetime free access

The results were immediate. Signups doubled overnight. People were sharing aggressively to secure their spot and compete for the top 10.

Key insight: Scarcity + competition = explosive growth. The leaderboard created a game out of referring friends.

End of Week 3: 1,543 signups, 48% from referrals.

Week 4: Pre-Launch Prep

With 1,500+ people on the waitlist, I shifted focus to converting them into Day 1 supporters.

The "Get Ready" Email

One week before launch, I sent an email to the entire list:

  • Announced the exact launch date and time
  • Explained how Product Hunt upvotes work
  • Asked them to set a calendar reminder
  • Gave them a personal "thank you" for being an early supporter

The Launch Eve Message

The night before launch, I sent one final email:

  • "We're live in 12 hours!"
  • Direct link to the Product Hunt page (scheduled post)
  • Simple ask: "Click the link, leave an upvote, and leave a comment if you have time"

End of Week 4: 2,341 signups, 52% from referrals.

Launch Day: The Results

I launched on a Tuesday (statistically the best day for Product Hunt). Here's what happened:

  • 6:00 AM PT: Product goes live. Sent final email to waitlist.
  • 7:00 AM: 100 upvotes. Mostly from my waitlist.
  • 9:00 AM: Hit #2 Product of the Day. Comments are rolling in.
  • 12:00 PM: 500 upvotes. Featured in Product Hunt newsletter.
  • 6:00 PM: 700 upvotes. Holding steady at #3.
  • Midnight: Finished at #3 Product of the Day with 847 upvotes.

The conversion rate from waitlist to upvote was 34%. That's incredibly high — most launches see 5-10%.

What I Learned

1. Start building your waitlist 4+ weeks before launch

Four weeks was the minimum. If I could do it again, I'd start 6-8 weeks out. More time = more signups = more Day 1 support.

2. Referral loops are everything

Without the referral system, I would've had maybe 500 signups. The viral mechanics 4x'd my results.

3. Personal outreach beats mass marketing

The DMs I sent to indie hackers converted 10x better than my tweets. Personal > broadcast.

4. Scarcity creates urgency

The "limited spots" messaging was the single biggest driver of signups in Week 3.

5. Your waitlist is your launch army

These weren't random strangers — they were people who believed in my product before it existed. They showed up because I gave them a reason to care.
