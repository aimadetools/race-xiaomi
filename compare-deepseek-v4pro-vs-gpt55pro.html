Frequently Asked Questions

Is DeepSeek V4 Pro or GPT-5.5 Pro cheaper?

DeepSeek V4 Pro is dramatically cheaper at $0.435 input and $0.87 output per 1M tokens, compared to GPT-5.5 Pro at $30.00 input and $180.00 output. GPT-5.5 Pro is roughly 69x more expensive on input and 207x more expensive on output.

Can DeepSeek V4 Pro really replace GPT-5.5 Pro?

For many workloads, yes. DeepSeek V4 Pro handles coding, analysis, chatbot, and RAG tasks with quality comparable to models several times its price. GPT-5.5 Pro excels at the most complex multi-step reasoning and frontier-level tasks. If your use case doesn't require absolute cutting-edge capability, DeepSeek V4 Pro delivers exceptional value at 1/69th the cost.

What's the quality difference between DeepSeek V4 Pro and GPT-5.5 Pro?

GPT-5.5 Pro is OpenAI's most capable model, designed for frontier-level reasoning, complex multi-step tasks, and the most demanding AI workloads. DeepSeek V4 Pro is highly competitive on benchmarks for coding, analysis, and general conversation — often within a few percentage points of frontier models. For 90% of real-world applications, the quality gap doesn't justify a 69x price premium.

Do both models have the same context window?

Both offer approximately 1 million tokens of context. DeepSeek V4 Pro has a 1M context window, and GPT-5.5 Pro has 1.05M. The practical difference is negligible — both can handle extremely long documents, large codebases, or extensive conversation histories.

How much can I save by choosing DeepSeek V4 Pro over GPT-5.5 Pro?

The savings are enormous. With 5,000 requests/day at 2,000 input and 500 output tokens, GPT-5.5 Pro costs $123,000/month while DeepSeek V4 Pro costs about $1,785/month — a savings of roughly $121,215/month or $1.45 million/year. Even at modest usage levels, the cost difference is transformative.

Is there a catch with DeepSeek V4 Pro's low pricing?

DeepSeek V4 Pro's pricing reflects a different business model — DeepSeek is a Chinese AI company that competes primarily on cost efficiency. The model is excellent for most workloads but may lag behind GPT-5.5 Pro on the most complex frontier tasks. Consider factors like data residency requirements, latency from different server locations, and whether your use case needs absolute cutting-edge reasoning. For most production workloads, the value proposition is outstanding.
